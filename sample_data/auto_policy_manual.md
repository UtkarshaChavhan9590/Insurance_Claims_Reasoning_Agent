# Auto Insurance Policy Manual — Coverage Guide

## Section 1: Collision Coverage
Collision coverage pays for damage to the policyholder's vehicle resulting from a
collision with another vehicle or object, regardless of fault. Standard deductible
is $500 unless a different deductible is specified on the policy declarations page.

Claims involving collision damage under $2,000 in repair estimates may be processed
through expedited review. Claims at or above $2,000 require senior adjuster review
before payout authorization.

## Section 2: Comprehensive Coverage
Comprehensive coverage pays for damage not caused by collision, including theft,
vandalism, fire, falling objects, and weather events (hail, flooding). Standard
deductible is $250.

## Section 3: Exclusions
The following are NOT covered under standard policy terms:
- Damage occurring while the vehicle is used for commercial ridesharing, unless
  the policyholder has the Rideshare Endorsement (Form RS-100) attached.
- Damage resulting from intentional acts by the policyholder.
- Mechanical breakdown or wear-and-tear (covered separately under extended warranty).
- Damage occurring while driving under the influence; such claims are automatically
  flagged for fraud review and require law enforcement report attachment.

## Section 4: Required Documentation
For collision claims, the following documents are required before a claim can be
approved:
1. Police report (if applicable, required for claims over $1,000)
2. Photos of all damaged areas
3. Repair estimate from a licensed shop
4. Driver's statement describing the incident

Claims missing required documentation should be routed back to the claimant with
a specific list of missing items, not denied outright.

## Section 5: Fraud Indicators
Claims adjusters and automated systems should flag claims for fraud review if any
of the following are present:
- Claim filed within 14 days of policy inception
- Reported damage inconsistent with the described incident
- Multiple claims from the same policyholder within a 6-month period
- Repair estimate significantly exceeds the vehicle's market value
Flagged claims must be reviewed by a human fraud analyst before any payout decision.

## Section 6: Approval Authority Limits
- Claims under $2,000: may be auto-approved by the AI claims agent if all
  documentation is present and no fraud indicators are triggered.
- Claims $2,000 and above: require human adjuster sign-off regardless of
  documentation completeness.
- Claims with any fraud indicator present: require human fraud analyst sign-off
  regardless of claim amount.

# Home / Property Insurance — Coverage Guide

## Section H1: Water Damage Coverage
Water damage caused by sudden and accidental discharge (e.g. burst pipe, appliance
overflow) is covered. Gradual leaks, seepage, or flooding from external bodies of
water are NOT covered unless a flood endorsement is attached. Standard deductible
is $1,000.

Claims under $2,000 may be auto-approved if all documentation is complete and no
fraud indicators are present. Claims at or above $2,000 require human adjuster review.

## Section H2: Fire and Smoke Damage
Accidental fire and smoke damage to the insured property is covered. Intentional
acts or arson are excluded. Standard deductible is $1,000.

## Section H3: Theft and Vandalism
Theft and vandalism are covered subject to a police report being filed within
48 hours of discovery. Standard deductible is $500.

## Section H4: Required Documentation (Home)
1. Damage photographs covering all affected areas
2. Contractor repair estimate from a licensed contractor
3. Proof of ownership (deed, mortgage statement, or title)
4. Incident report (filed with local authorities or building management)
5. Police report (mandatory for theft and vandalism claims)
Temporary repair receipts should be submitted to claim reimbursement for emergency
mitigation costs.

## Section H5: Exclusions (Home)
- Gradual deterioration, wear and tear, mold from chronic moisture
- Earthquake and flood damage (separate endorsements required)
- Damage to vacant properties (unoccupied > 30 consecutive days)
- Intentional damage by the policyholder or resident family members

## Section H6: Fraud Indicators (Home)
- Claim filed within 30 days of policy inception
- Repair estimate from an unlicensed or unverifiable contractor
- Multiple property claims in a 12-month period
- No third-party corroboration (neighbor, fire department, utility company)

# Health / Medical Insurance — Coverage Guide

## Section M1: Hospitalization Coverage
Inpatient hospitalization at an accredited hospital or clinic is covered including
room charges, surgery fees, anesthesia, and post-operative care. Standard
deductible is $250 per admission.

Claims under $2,000 may be auto-approved with complete documentation. Claims at
or above $2,000 require human adjuster review.

## Section M2: Outpatient and Emergency Coverage
Emergency room visits and outpatient procedures at accredited facilities are
covered. Non-emergency outpatient visits at in-network facilities are covered
at 80% after deductible. Out-of-network facilities require pre-authorization.

## Section M3: Exclusions (Health)
- Pre-existing conditions diagnosed before policy inception (12-month waiting
  period applies; waived after 24 months of continuous coverage)
- Cosmetic procedures not medically necessary
- Dental and vision (separate riders required)
- Experimental treatments not approved by a recognized medical body
- Treatment outside the policy's geographic coverage area without prior authorization

## Section M4: Required Documentation (Health)
1. Original hospital or clinic invoice with itemized charges
2. Attending physician's report or discharge summary
3. Prescription receipts (for medication reimbursement)
4. Medical certificate for extended leave claims
Copies are acceptable; originals must be available on request.

## Section M5: Fraud Indicators (Health)
- Claim filed within 14 days of policy inception
- Treatment dates outside the policy's active period
- Invoice from a non-accredited or unverifiable facility
- Multiple claims for the same diagnosis within a 6-month window

# Travel Insurance — Coverage Guide

## Section T1: Trip Cancellation Coverage
Non-refundable pre-paid travel expenses are reimbursed when a trip is cancelled
due to: airline cancellation, natural disaster, serious illness of the policyholder
or immediate family member, or involuntary job loss. Standard deductible is $200.

Claims under $2,000 may be auto-approved with complete documentation. Claims
at or above $2,000 require human adjuster review.

## Section T2: Baggage Loss and Delay
Loss or theft of checked baggage is covered up to the policy limit. Delay of
baggage for more than 12 hours triggers a per-diem allowance for essential
purchases. The airline's baggage loss report is mandatory.

## Section T3: Travel Medical Coverage
Emergency medical expenses incurred abroad are covered. Evacuation and
repatriation costs are included. Non-emergency treatment abroad requires
pre-authorization.

## Section T4: Required Documentation (Travel)
1. Airline or hotel cancellation confirmation (official document from carrier)
2. Original booking receipts showing pre-paid non-refundable amounts
3. Airline baggage loss report (for baggage claims)
4. Medical certificate from treating physician (for medical/cancellation due to illness)
5. Proof of travel dates (boarding passes, e-ticket confirmation)
Claims missing the airline or hotel cancellation confirmation cannot proceed to
approval; the claimant must be contacted for this document.

## Section T5: Exclusions (Travel)
- Pre-existing medical conditions (unless declared and approved at policy inception)
- Cancellations due to change of mind or voluntary decisions
- Travel to countries under active government advisories at time of booking
- Loss of cash, credit cards, or documents (covered under separate rider)

## Section T6: Fraud Indicators (Travel)
- Trip booked very close to travel date after policy inception for a near-future trip
- Claim filed for a condition declared as pre-existing at inception
- No third-party cancellation documentation from carrier
- Multiple travel claims within a 12-month period
